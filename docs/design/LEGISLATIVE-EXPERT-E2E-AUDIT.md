# Legislative Expert E2E Audit

This document records the deterministic answer-readiness gate for MOLEG-API as used by a future legislative-expert skill.

The executable harness is `scripts/legislative_expert_e2e_audit.py`.
The regression gate is `tests/test_legislative_expert_e2e_audit.py`.
The companion prompt-planning dry-run is `scripts/legislative_expert_prompt_dry_run.py`.
The crosswalk regression gate is `tests/test_legislative_expert_gate_crosswalk.py`.
The artifact-safety gate is `tests/test_legislative_expert_artifact_safety.py`.
The answer-discipline harness is `scripts/legislative_expert_answer_discipline.py`.

## Purpose

The live e2e gate proves that representative law.go.kr source families are reachable.
The fake-skill tracer bullet proves that public `MolegApi` methods can orchestrate source loading across recurring legislative workflows.
This audit adds one more layer: before the skill reasons or drafts an answer, it must preserve the answer-readiness packet shape:

- source citations with authority labels
- current-law basis versus promulgation bridge status
- loaded context versus candidates versus deferred follow-ups
- WebSearch gaps for non-MOLEG context
- `needs_more_source_loading` states when a source was only discovered as a candidate
- blocking guardrails for ambiguous or source-lagged legal identity
- risk flags that prevent overclaiming

The audit still does not generate legal conclusions. It verifies that the skill has enough structured source state to reason without silently guessing.

The prompt dry-run checks the preceding step: natural-language legislative prompts are mapped to public `MolegApi` interfaces, `congress-db` handoffs, WebSearch handoffs, guardrails, and forbidden shortcuts without leaking raw law.go.kr target values.

The crosswalk test checks that prompt plans and answer-readiness reports agree on terminal states for the highest-risk paths: proposed-bill blocking, enacted-bill change tracing after current-law bridge resolution, loaded before/after amendment-delta review, historical/repealed law review, supplementary-transition review, nested article unit completeness, deleted article status, moved article status, query-expansion candidate authority, law-search candidate detail loading, empty law-search absence claims, interpretation-search candidate detail loading, empty interpretation-search absence claims, source-access failure handling, case-search candidate detail loading, empty case-search absence claims, Constitutional Court search candidate detail loading, empty Constitutional Court-search absence claims, loaded authority article-reference matching, context-bundle authority article-reference mismatch, context-bundle unverified authority references, context-bundle partial authority article matches, context-bundle authority temporal mismatches, law-structure hierarchy loading, empty delegation-graph absence claims, administrative-rule search candidate detail loading, administrative-rule issued-on/as-of distinction, administrative-rule article status, administrative-rule supplementary transition, empty administrative-rule search absence claims, administrative-rule missing source-reference handling, comparable-mechanism candidate detail loading, annex/form search candidate detail loading, empty annex/form search absence claims, low-confidence annex/form extraction, interpretation authority distinction, future-effective promulgated law, candidate-only delegated criteria, follow-up-loaded delegated criteria, delegated-criteria source mismatch, future-effective administrative-rule detail, institutional-system explicit statute-set limits, Constitutional Court free-text doctrine search, comparable-mechanism source loading, and latest-social-context WebSearch handoff.

## Operating Notes

- This is deterministic and does not require `MOLEG_OC`.
- It uses fake law.go.kr-shaped payloads through a source adapter, then calls public `MolegApi` methods only.
- A scenario marked `needs_more_source_loading` is a success when it proves that candidate-only context remains uncited and is converted into explicit follow-up actions.
- A context-bundle authority scenario is a success when loaded interpretation/case/Constitutional Court details remain citable as bounded first-pass context, administrative-rule and annex/form candidates remain follow-up work, and neither state can become an exhaustive authority survey.
- A context-bundle authority-mismatch scenario is a success when `authority_article_mismatch` gaps preserve follow-up search requirements and eager-loaded authority details do not become target-article citations.
- A context-bundle authority-unverified scenario is a success when `authority_article_unverified` gaps preserve missing structured article references as unknown source state and prevent eager-loaded authority details from becoming target-article citations.
- A context-bundle authority-partial-match scenario is a success when `authority_article_partial_match` gaps preserve the missing requested article and prevent authority detail matched to one requested article from being broadcast to all requested articles.
- A context-bundle authority-temporal-mismatch scenario is a success when `authority_temporal_mismatch` gaps preserve authority dates older than the loaded article's effective date or missing/unparseable authority dates and prevent matching article references from becoming current-authority claims before history/as-of checks.
- A follow-up-loaded scenario is a success when selected administrative-rule and annex/form bodies are loaded through their detail interfaces before they appear as usable citations.
- A delegated-criteria source-mismatch scenario is a success when loaded administrative-rule detail stays follow-up context and does not become target operational criteria unless its explicit source law/article matches the target statute/article.
- A latest-social-context WebSearch scenario is a success when MOLEG legal text remains citable as legal authority but no latest statistic, news, or social-fact claim is allowed until WebSearch has loaded separate sources.
- A supplementary-transition scenario is a success when `supplementary_provisions` are citable separately and the skill cannot answer 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치 from main-article text or law-level `effective_date` metadata alone.
- A nested-article-unit scenario is a success when `ArticleText.text` preserves source 항, 호, and 목 text and the skill is forbidden from answering definition/application-target questions from `조문제목` or top-level `조문내용` alone.
- A deleted-article scenario is a success when `ArticleText.is_deleted` and related status metadata prevent the skill from treating `제N조 삭제` as operative current text.
- A moved-article scenario is a success when `ArticleText.moved_to` is preserved as source state and `load_article_context()` loads the destination article before any current-substance citation.
- A query-expansion scenario is a success when law/term/related-article candidates produce follow-up searches but zero usable citations until selected source text is loaded.
- A loaded before/after amendment-delta scenario is a success when `compare_law_versions()` supports a selected article wording-delta claim but cannot become amendment reason, legislative intent, full bill purpose, or exhaustive changed-provision coverage.
- A law-search scenario is a success when `search_laws()` preserves identity candidates but produces zero usable law-text or article citations until selected source text is loaded.
- An empty law-search scenario is a success when zero hits stay scoped to the exact query/effective-basis that was searched and cannot become a no-current-law or no-legal-basis claim.
- An interpretation-search scenario is a success when `search_interpretations()` preserves candidate metadata and authority labels but produces zero usable interpretation-substance citations until `get_interpretation()` loads selected detail.
- An empty interpretation-search scenario is a success when zero hits stay scoped to the exact query/source that was searched and cannot become a no-interpretation or no-authority claim.
- A source-access failure scenario is a success when `RateLimitError` or `RetryExhaustedError` stays distinct from a zero-hit search result and cannot become a no-current-law or no-source claim.
- A case-search scenario is a success when `search_cases()` preserves candidate metadata but produces zero usable judicial citations until `get_case()` loads selected detail.
- An empty case-search scenario is a success when zero hits stay scoped to the exact query/court/body scope that was searched and cannot become a no-precedent, no-court-case, no-judicial-authority, or no-legal-reasoning claim.
- A constitutional-search scenario is a success when `search_constitutional_decisions()` preserves candidate metadata but produces zero usable Constitutional Court citations until `get_constitutional_decision()` loads selected detail.
- An empty Constitutional Court-search scenario is a success when zero hits stay scoped to the exact query/body scope that was searched and cannot become a no-constitutional-decision, no-constitutional-authority, or no-constitutional-risk claim.

- An empty delegation-graph scenario is a success when `find_delegated_rules()` returning `NoResultError` stays scoped to the searched law/article and cannot become a no-delegated-rule or no-subordinate-rule claim.
- An administrative-rule-search scenario is a success when `search_administrative_rules()` preserves candidate metadata but produces zero usable administrative-rule citations until `get_administrative_rule()` loads selected detail.

- An interpretation authority scenario is a success when MOLEG official and ministry first-instance interpretation labels survive into citations and cannot be collapsed into one authority level.
- A scenario marked `blocked_for_manual_review` is a success when it proves that MOLEG-API prevents a bad legal conclusion.
- A proposed bill without `congress-db` promulgation bridge fields must stay blocked; a bill title is not a current-law identity.
- Prompt dry-runs must use public `MolegApi` method names, not raw law.go.kr target values.
- Audit artifacts must stay JSON-native; avoid tuples or other Python-only shapes in report evidence.
- Answer-discipline reports do not draft legal conclusions; they constrain what the future skill may claim before it writes.
- The audit complements live tests; it cannot prove that current live law.go.kr data still behaves the same.
- If a future skill prompt starts answering from candidate data without loading selected sources, add that workflow here as a failing e2e scenario before changing implementation.
