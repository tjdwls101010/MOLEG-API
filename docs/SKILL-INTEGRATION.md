# MOLEG-API Skill Integration

This document describes how the future legislative-expert skill should use MOLEG-API alongside `congress-db` and WebSearch. It is a living integration guide; update it when public interfaces or source traps change.

The intended user is Claude with a legislative-expert skill loaded. MOLEG-API should therefore return normalized legal context that can be inserted into reasoning, citations, and follow-up calls, not raw endpoint payloads that force the skill prompt to memorize law.go.kr trivia.

For package installation, serialization examples, vendored fallback, and skill-runtime error handling, use `docs/SKILL-AUTHOR-COOKBOOK.md`.
For deterministic answer-readiness verification, use `docs/design/LEGISLATIVE-EXPERT-E2E-AUDIT.md` and `scripts/legislative_expert_e2e_audit.py`.

## Source Responsibilities

Use `congress-db` for National Assembly facts:
- bills and bill versions
- sponsors and committees
- votes
- minutes and meeting-bill links
- promulgation bridge fields: `prom_law_nm`, `prom_no`, `promulgation_dt`

The live `congress-db` schema was introspected through the read-only `congress_ro` Neon role. The current evidence is stored in `docs/design/congress-db-introspection/`. The bridge fields live in `public.bill_final_outcomes`, joined to `public.bills` by stable `bill_no`.

Use MOLEG-API for legal sources from law.go.kr:
- current statutes and articles
- promulgation-date and effective-date law identity resolution
- law history and before/after comparison
- delegated enforcement decrees, enforcement rules, notices, and administrative rules
- annex/form candidates and selected text bodies for statutes and administrative rules
- MOLEG official interpretations and ministry first-instance interpretations
- Supreme Court cases and Constitutional Court decisions
- law terms, related terms, related articles, related laws, query expansion, and comparable-mechanism discovery

Use WebSearch for facts outside MOLEG's legal corpus:
- latest social context
- statistics
- news
- policy announcements
- recent government reports
- non-legal background evidence

## Progressive Loading Rules

Use MOLEG-API in layers instead of asking one call to load everything:

1. Start with candidate/planning interfaces such as `search_laws()`, `expand_legal_query()`, `find_comparable_mechanisms()`, `search_administrative_rules()`, `search_annex_forms()`, `search_interpretations()`, and judicial searches.
2. Load full text only for sources that are likely to matter: `get_law()`, `get_article()`, `load_article_context()`, `get_administrative_rule()`, `load_administrative_rule_context()`, `get_annex_form_body()`, `get_interpretation()`, `get_case()`, `get_constitutional_decision()`, or `load_authority_context()`.
3. Use `load_legal_context_bundle()` as a staged first pass when the question is broad or under-specified. Treat its candidates and deferred lookups as the next menu, not as proof that every relevant source body has been inspected.
4. Use `load_institutional_system()` after the skill has an explicit statute set for one 제도. It composes those statutes into one staged bundle; it does not discover the set or decide which statute is primary.

Do not optimize for the absolute smallest number of public methods. A single maximal API wastes context and hides source-choice decisions. Do not optimize for one method per source endpoint either. The right public method is one Claude can choose by legislative intent.

Do not treat unused law.go.kr endpoints as missing context. If a source is optional, demand-gated, customized, local-only, or outside the user's legislative question, leave it alone until it prevents answering a concrete question.

If only `search_laws()` has run, do not cite current law text, article wording, duties, sanctions, or procedures. Search hits are identity candidates; load selected law/article detail first, then cite the loaded source text.

If only `expand_legal_query()` has run, do not cite legal-term, related-law, related-article, or AI-search candidates as legal authority. Load selected law/article detail first, then cite the loaded source text.

## Default Workflow From A Promulgated Bill

1. Query `congress-db` for the bill and its promulgation bridge fields.
2. Call `resolve_promulgated_law(prom_law_nm=..., prom_no=..., promulgation_dt=...)`.
3. If the result is ambiguous, surface the candidates instead of choosing silently.
4. Call `get_law(..., basis="effective")` or `get_article(..., basis="effective")` to inspect the text currently in force. For bundle-based bill checks, pass `as_of=<reference date>` to `load_legal_context_bundle()` and inspect any `not_effective_as_of` gap before saying the law is currently in force.
5. Call `trace_law_history()` or `compare_law_versions()` to explain what the bill changed. Resolving the promulgation bridge and loading current text proves identity/current wording, not the amendment delta. When `congress-db` already returned the enacting `bill_id`, pass a `promulgation_bridge` map so matching `HistoryEvent` rows carry that `bill_id`.
6. Call `find_delegated_rules()` to inspect article-level delegation to enforcement decrees, enforcement rules, notices, and administrative rules.
7. Call `get_law_structure()` when the task needs the broader `lsStmd` law hierarchy across statutes, enforcement instruments, and administrative rules; do not expect article-level `source_article` links from this structure.
8. Call `search_administrative_rules()` and `get_administrative_rule()` when delegated or practical execution criteria may live in notices, directives, established rules, or other administrative rules. Use `load_administrative_rule_context()` when deleted or moved administrative-rule articles could affect current operational criteria.
9. Call `search_annex_forms()` when the legal question may depend on attached tables, thresholds, amounts, criteria, application formats, or other 별표ㆍ서식 material. Call `get_annex_form_body()` for selected candidates before treating the attached content as inspected.
10. Call `search_interpretations()` and `search_cases()` when legal meaning, application constraints, or constitutional risk matter.
11. Use WebSearch only for current social facts or context outside law.go.kr.

## Query Planning Rules

- Prefer effective-date basis for "current law", "now in force", and "현재 시행" questions.
- Use promulgation-date basis when resolving a `congress-db` promulgation bridge or reconstructing historical promulgation context.
- A promulgation bridge is not current-force proof. For "현재 시행" questions, carry an explicit reference date with `as_of` and disclose a `not_effective_as_of` gap when the loaded effective date is later.
- A current-law bridge packet is not amendment-delta proof. Before saying which provisions the bill changed, run `trace_law_history()` or `compare_law_versions()` for the relevant promulgation event, article, or date range.
- `compare_law_versions()` / `oldAndNew` supports before/after wording deltas for loaded rows. It does not prove amendment reason, legislative intent, full bill purpose, or exhaustive changed-provision coverage; use `trace_law_history()` and `congress-db` bill materials when those claims matter.
- For 구법, 폐지법, or "당시" questions, treat the task as historical source loading. Use history/promulgated discovery and `get_article(..., as_of=<historical date>)` or `get_law(..., as_of=<historical date>)`; do not answer from current effective search alone. A current no-result is not proof that the historical source never existed.
- Use `trace_law_history()` without article/date filters when law-level amendment chronology matters. It parses the HTML-only `lsHistory` list table into normalized metadata events. Use `trace_law_history(article=...)` when the wording evolution of one provision matters; article-scoped events may include `article_text` for the post-change snapshot, while full-law history keeps `article_text=None`.
- Treat `HistoryEvent.promulgation_law_name`, `HistoryEvent.promulgation_number`, and `HistoryEvent.promulgation_date` as the bridge keys for joining back to `congress-db.public.bill_final_outcomes(prom_law_nm, prom_no, promulgation_dt)`. `HistoryEvent.bill_id` is populated only when the caller supplies a bridge map; MOLEG-API does not query `congress-db` directly.
- Treat law-name search as candidate discovery. Multiple plausible results are an ambiguity, not permission to pick the first hit.
- Use `expand_legal_query()` for search planning, not as final legal authority; its follow-up searches can include annex/form discovery before WebSearch handoff. Its legal-term, related-law, related-article, and AI-search candidates are planning context until selected source text is loaded.
- Treat annex/form search as candidate discovery. It exposes metadata and file/detail links; call `get_annex_form_body()` for a selected law/admin-rule candidate when the attached table, threshold, amount, criterion, or form may be operative. Do not cite thresholds, amounts, criteria, form content, or extracted rows from `search_annex_forms()` alone. If `AnnexFormText.structured_data` is present, use `parsing_confidence` before relying on rows; the plain `text` remains the fallback source for irregular or low-confidence tables.
- Treat an empty `search_annex_forms()` result as scoped query/source/type/scope evidence only. It can support "this exact annex/form search returned zero hits," but not "no attached criteria, annex, form, threshold table, amount criterion, or attached material exists."
- Treat administrative-rule `source_law_id`, `source_law_name`, `source_article`, and `source_article_title` as source-provided back-references only. If they are `None`, the correct interpretation is "not exposed in this MOLEG payload"; do not infer that the rule has no authorizing statute.
- Treat `get_law_structure()` as hierarchy context. It can show that enforcement instruments or administrative rules sit under a statute, but it does not prove article-level delegation, lower-rule body text, or operational criteria. Use `find_delegated_rules()`, selected statute articles, and selected lower-rule detail before making those claims.
- Treat an empty `find_delegated_rules()` result or `NoResultError` as scoped law/article evidence only. It can support "this exact delegation lookup returned no rules," but not "no delegated rule, subordinate rule, notice, annex, or delegated criteria exists."
- Treat administrative-rule search as candidate discovery. Search hits can preserve identity/date/source-reference metadata, but article text, criteria, supplementary provisions, and current operational criteria require selected `get_administrative_rule()` detail loading. `search_administrative_rules(issued_on=...)` filters 발령일자, not 시행일자, so the skill must compare `AdministrativeRuleText.identity.effective_date` to the answer's reference date before saying the selected notice/directive/rule states current operational criteria.
- Treat administrative-rule supplementary provisions as separate source text. For 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치 in a notice/directive/rule, use `AdministrativeRuleText.supplementary_provisions`; do not answer from the selected article text or rule-level `effective_date` metadata alone.
- Use `find_comparable_mechanisms()` when the task is legislative design or comparative 제도 discovery, such as "find statutes with similar 과징금, 인허가, 신고제, authorization, or sanction structures." Treat the returned `LawIdentity` values as planning candidates with article/source provenance; load the selected law or article before citing legal structure, claiming legal equivalence, or judging design suitability.
- Treat constitutional doctrines such as `과잉금지원칙` or `평등원칙` as free-text search terms, not structured filters. The law.go.kr `detc` source does not expose a doctrine/category field, so `search_constitutional_decisions(search_body=True)` can find candidate decisions by keyword but cannot prove doctrine-indexed coverage.
- Treat `search_constitutional_decisions()` output as Constitutional Court candidate metadata. Load selected detail with `get_constitutional_decision()` before citing 판시사항, 결정요지, 심판대상조문, 참조조문, reviewed-statute reasoning, or full text.
- Preserve source authority labels in answers: MOLEG interpretation, ministry interpretation, Supreme Court case, and Constitutional Court decision are different source types. For interpretations, `source="all"` means MOLEG plus one specified ministry; use `source="all_ministries"` only when registry-wide ministry fan-out is intentional.
- Treat an empty `search_interpretations()`, `search_cases()`, `search_constitutional_decisions()`, `search_administrative_rules()`, or `search_annex_forms()` result as a scoped query/source result, not proof that no relevant authority, rule, or attached material exists. Before making any absence claim, disclose the searched terms/source scope and consider alternate terms, source families, or detail loaders required by the question.
- Treat an empty `search_cases()` result as scoped query/court/body evidence only. It can support "this exact case search returned zero hits," but not "no relevant precedent, court case, judicial authority, or judicial reasoning exists."
- Treat an empty `search_constitutional_decisions()` result as scoped query/body evidence only. It can support "this exact Constitutional Court search returned zero hits," but not "no constitutional decision, constitutional authority, or constitutional risk exists."

## Fallback Rules

- If MOLEG-API cannot answer because the needed source is outside law.go.kr, use WebSearch.
- If MOLEG-API finds no law for a bill that has no promulgation bridge, return to `congress-db` and treat the bill as not proven enacted/current.
- If a `congress-db` promulgation bridge does not exactly resolve but the bundle returns law-name candidates with `source_lag_or_manual_review_required`, explain the source-lag/manual-review state instead of saying the bill was not enacted.
- If a promulgation bridge resolves but the bundle returns `not_effective_as_of`, explain that the law is promulgated/source-loadable but not effective on the reference date instead of calling it current law.
- If MOLEG-API raises `RateLimitError` or `RetryExhaustedError`, treat it as a temporary source-access problem, not proof that the legal source does not exist.
- If a source endpoint is HTML-only, use the documented parser/fallback for that interface; do not assume JSON exists. `lsHistory` is supported only through its list table parser, not by treating the law.go.kr UI iframe as stable structured data.
- If a law delegates details to lower rules, do not stop at statute text unless the user explicitly asks for statute-only review.
- If `find_delegated_rules()` raises `NoResultError`, disclose the searched law/article scope and continue through law structure, alternate article scopes, administrative-rule candidates, or annex/form paths before saying no delegated rule or subordinate source exists.
- If `search_administrative_rules()` returns zero hits, disclose the exact searched query/filter scope and continue through statute-level delegation, law structure, alternate rule names, or annex/form paths before saying no delegated criteria or subordinate rule exists.
- If `search_annex_forms()` returns zero hits, disclose the searched query, source, annex/form type, and search scope. Continue through source law text, administrative-rule candidates, alternate annex/form terms, administrative-rule annex/forms, or selected detail paths before saying no attached criteria or form exists.
- If `search_cases()` returns zero hits, disclose the query, court scope, and whether title/body search was used. Try alternate legal terms, statute names, article numbers, broader court/body scopes, Constitutional Court decisions, or interpretations before saying no relevant precedent or judicial authority exists.
- If `search_constitutional_decisions()` returns zero hits, disclose the free-text query/body scope. Try alternate doctrine terms, reviewed-statute terms, ordinary court cases, or interpretations before saying no constitutional decision, constitutional authority, or constitutional risk exists.
- If a prompt asks about definitions, application targets, exceptions, or requirements, use loaded `ArticleText.text` / `AdministrativeRuleArticleText.text` because MOLEG-API preserves nested 항, 호, and 목 there. Do not answer from `조문제목` or top-level `조문내용` alone.
- If a loaded article has `is_deleted=True` or `revision_type="삭제"`, cite it only as deleted source state. Do not treat `제N조 삭제` as current operative duties, permissions, sanctions, or procedures.
- If a loaded article has `moved_to` or `revision_type="이동"`, cite the movement marker only as source state and use `load_article_context()` or a destination-article load before describing current duties, permissions, sanctions, procedures, or requirements.
- If a loaded administrative-rule article has `is_deleted=True`, `revision_type="삭제"`, `moved_to`, or `revision_type="이동"`, cite it only as source state; use `load_administrative_rule_context()` before describing current operational criteria from a moved destination article.
- If a selected administrative rule has `supplementary_provisions`, cite those rule 부칙 separately for 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치. Do not merge them into the administrative-rule article citation or treat `AdministrativeRuleText.identity.effective_date` as the full transition analysis.
- If a selected administrative-rule body loads but its `effective_date` is after the reference date, cite it only as future-effective/source-loaded text and look for the currently effective administrative-rule text before describing current operational criteria.
- If a constitutional-risk answer relies on `detc` search, disclose that doctrine terms were free-text search terms. Do not claim exhaustive Constitutional Court doctrine coverage until deferred candidates and alternate terms have been reviewed. Do not cite decision substance from search hits alone; load selected detail through `get_constitutional_decision()`.
- If a result points to annex/form files that likely carry the operative criteria, run `get_annex_form_body()` for the selected candidate or surface the remaining direct HWP/PDF limitation instead of pretending statute text is complete.

## Context Bundle

The bundle contract for Claude is in `docs/design/LEGAL-CONTEXT-BUNDLE.md`. `MolegApi.load_legal_context_bundle()` implements staged loading: statutes/articles first, delegated context next, administrative-rule and annex/form candidates when lower-rule or attached material may matter, interpretation and judicial context as bounded candidates with selective full-text loading, `authority_article_mismatch` gaps when eager-loaded authority details point to different requested articles, `authority_article_unverified` gaps when structured article references are missing, `authority_article_partial_match` gaps when eager-loaded authority details match only some requested articles, and explicit WebSearch gaps for latest social context.
`MolegApi.load_institutional_system()` uses the same bundle shape for multi-statute 제도 review when Claude already selected the statutes. It loads statute text/articles, law structures, and delegations for each statute, then leaves administrative-rule, annex/form, interpretation, case, and constitutional detail as candidates or deferred follow-ups. Pass `as_of` when the explicit statute set is being reviewed as current law; inspect `not_effective_as_of` before calling every statute in the set currently effective.
Do not claim exhaustive interpretation, case, Constitutional Court, administrative-rule, or annex/form coverage from a context bundle unless the deferred/detail searches needed for that coverage were loaded and disclosed.
Do not present `load_institutional_system()` output as exhaustive discovery of every statute in the broader institution unless a separate discovery step such as `expand_legal_query()` / `search_laws()` was run and disclosed.

## E2E Readiness Gates

Use these gates together when validating the future legislative-expert skill:

- `tests/test_live_e2e_scenarios.py` checks live law.go.kr source reachability across representative legal-source families. It requires `MOLEG_OC`.
- `scripts/fake_skill_tracer_bullet.py` checks deterministic public-interface orchestration across recurring legislative workflow archetypes.
- `scripts/legislative_expert_e2e_audit.py` checks deterministic answer-readiness packets: citations, authority labels, current-law basis, candidates, deferred lookups, WebSearch gaps, more-source-loading states, and blocking guardrails.
- `scripts/legislative_expert_prompt_dry_run.py` checks natural-language prompt planning before source loading: public `MolegApi` method selection, `congress-db` first steps, WebSearch handoffs, guardrails, and forbidden shortcuts.
- `scripts/legislative_expert_answer_discipline.py` checks the final pre-answer claim discipline: allowed claims, forbidden claims, required disclosures, required follow-ups, and usable citations.

The answer-readiness audit is the closest pre-skill source gate. A `needs_more_source_loading` report is successful when it prevents the skill from citing candidate-only context as inspected source text. A query-expansion report is successful when legal-term, related-law, related-article, and AI-search candidates stay as planning context with zero usable citations until selected source text is loaded. A law-search report is successful when law-name hits stay as identity candidates with zero usable law-text or article citations until selected source text is loaded. A source-access failure report is successful when `RateLimitError` or `RetryExhaustedError` stays distinct from zero-hit source results and cannot become a no-current-law or no-source claim. A case-search report is successful when court-case search hits stay as candidate metadata with zero usable judicial citations until `get_case()` loads selected detail. An empty case-search report is successful when zero hits remain scoped to the searched query/court/body scope and cannot become a no-precedent, no-court-case, no-judicial-authority, or no-legal-reasoning claim. A constitutional-search report is successful when Constitutional Court search hits stay as candidate metadata with zero usable decision-substance citations until `get_constitutional_decision()` loads selected detail. An empty Constitutional Court-search report is successful when zero hits remain scoped to the searched query/body scope and cannot become a no-constitutional-decision, no-constitutional-authority, or no-constitutional-risk claim. A law-structure report is successful when hierarchy nodes remain citable only as hierarchy context and cannot become article-level delegation, lower-rule body text, or operational-criteria proof. An administrative-rule-search report is successful when administrative-rule search hits stay as candidate metadata with zero usable rule-text citations until `get_administrative_rule()` loads selected detail. An administrative-rule issued-on/as-of report is successful when `search_administrative_rules(issued_on=...)` stays 발령일자 candidate discovery and cannot become effective-date current-operational proof before selected detail loading. An administrative-rule article-status report is successful when `load_administrative_rule_context()` preserves deleted/moved source state, loads the moved destination article before criteria claims, and cites current operational criteria only from `current_articles`. An administrative-rule supplementary-transition report is successful when loaded rule 부칙 stays separately citable and the skill cannot answer 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치 from administrative-rule article text or rule-level `effective_date` metadata alone. An empty administrative-rule-search report is successful when zero hits remain scoped to the searched query/filter and cannot become a no-delegated-criteria, no-subordinate-rule, no-notice, or no-annex claim. An administrative-rule missing-source-reference report is successful when loaded administrative-rule text remains citable but absent `source_law_name` / `source_article` metadata stays an unknown payload state, not proof of no authorization, no delegation, or invalidity. A comparable-mechanism report is successful when `find_comparable_mechanisms()` preserves law/article-anchor planning candidates with zero usable comparison citations until selected article text is loaded. An annex/form-search report is successful when annex/form search hits stay as candidate metadata with zero usable attached-body citations until `get_annex_form_body()` loads selected detail. An empty annex/form-search report is successful when zero hits remain scoped to the searched query/source/type/scope and cannot become a no-attached-criteria, no-annex, no-form, no-threshold-table, or no-attached-material claim. A context-bundle authority report is successful when loaded interpretation, case, and Constitutional Court details remain citable as bounded first-pass context, administrative-rule and annex/form candidates remain follow-up work, and none of that can become an exhaustive authority survey or absence-of-authority claim. A context-bundle authority-mismatch report is successful when `authority_article_mismatch` gaps preserve follow-up search requirements and eager-loaded authority details do not become target-article citations. A context-bundle authority-unverified report is successful when `authority_article_unverified` gaps preserve missing structured article references as unknown source state and eager-loaded authority details do not become target-article citations. A context-bundle authority-partial-match report is successful when `authority_article_partial_match` gaps preserve the missing requested article and eager-loaded authority detail matched to one requested article cannot be broadcast to all requested articles. A latest-social-context WebSearch report is successful when MOLEG legal text remains citable but latest statistics, news, announcements, and social facts stay unanswered until WebSearch loads separate sources. A supplementary-transition report is successful when loaded `supplementary_provisions` remain separate from main articles and law-level `effective_date` metadata. A nested-article-unit report is successful when loaded article text preserves 항, 호, and 목 and the skill cannot answer definition/application-target prompts from the article title or top-level article content alone. A deleted-article report is successful when loaded article status marks the article deleted and the skill cannot cite the deletion marker as current operative text. A moved-article report is successful when `load_article_context()` preserves the movement marker as source state and loads the destination article before any current-substance citation. A follow-up-loaded `ready_for_reasoning` report is successful when selected administrative-rule and annex/form bodies were loaded through their detail interfaces before citation. A low-confidence annex/form report is successful when retained plain text can be cited but empty/low-confidence structured rows cannot become a threshold claim or a "no criteria" claim. A future-effective administrative-rule report is successful when selected detail remains citable as loaded source text but cannot be described as current operational criteria. A current-law bridge report is successful when it allows citation of resolved current wording but still forbids amendment-delta claims until history/diff has been loaded. A loaded before/after amendment-delta report is successful when `compare_law_versions()` supports the selected article's wording delta but cannot become amendment reason, legislative intent, full bill purpose, or exhaustive changed-provision coverage. A historical/repealed-law report is successful when historical text carries an explicit as-of date and preserved history/repeal status, while still being forbidden as current-law authority. A Constitutional Court free-text report is successful when loaded `detc` decisions can be cited but cannot become exhaustive doctrine coverage. A future-effective law report is successful when it allows the skill to say "promulgated but not effective as of the reference date" while forbidding a current-force claim. A `blocked_for_manual_review` report is successful when it prevents the skill from treating a proposed bill title as current law, silently selecting an ambiguous statute, or treating a MOLEG bridge mismatch as proof that a bill was not enacted.

A loaded-authority article-mismatch report is successful when selected interpretation, court-case, and Constitutional Court details remain loaded source state but produce zero target-article citations until `referenced_articles` or `reviewed_articles` match the requested law/article.

These interfaces are implemented across the initial core slices. Law and administrative-rule article loaders preserve source 항, 호, and 목 text inside `ArticleText.text` / `AdministrativeRuleArticleText.text`, so definition, application-target, exception, and requirement analysis does not require raw payload parsing. Article loaders also expose source article status fields (`article_kind`, `revision_type`, `moved_from`, `moved_to`, `has_changes`, `is_deleted`) so deleted or moved articles are not mistaken for current operative text; `load_article_context()` follows moved statute articles to their destination article by default and keeps destination-load failures as gaps/deferred lookups, and `load_administrative_rule_context()` does the same for selected administrative-rule articles while exposing only loaded destination rows in `current_articles`. Law and administrative-rule detail loaders expose source 부칙 as `supplementary_provisions`, so 시행일, 적용례, and 경과조치 analysis does not require raw payload parsing. Administrative-rule search uses source `admrul` but exposes `issued_on` rather than `as_of` because the catalog filter is 발령일자, not a true effective-date basis; selected administrative-rule detail preserves `effective_date`, which the skill must compare to its reference date before making current-operational-criteria claims. Annex/form search uses `licbyl` and `admbyl` internally while exposing task terms such as `source`, `search_scope`, and `annex_type`; selected bodies load through `get_annex_form_body()` text-export calls rather than direct HWP/PDF parsing, with optional conservative `structured_data` for clear table-like text exports. Interpretation search uses official `expc` and registry-backed ministry `*CgmExpc` targets while preserving source authority labels; `source="all"` means MOLEG plus one specified ministry, while `source="all_ministries"` performs an explicit high-cost fan-out across the ministry registry. The implementation normalizes live `Expc.expc` and `CgmExpc.cgmExpc` list wrappers so Claude does not need to know those source shapes. Case search uses `prec`; Constitutional Court decision search uses `detc`. Query expansion uses legal terms, everyday terms, related terms/articles/laws, AI search surfaces, and annex/form follow-up recommendations as planning hints only. Comparable-mechanism discovery uses `aiSearch`, `aiRltLs`, and `lstrmRltJo` to return bounded law candidates with endpoint/article provenance, not a ranked 제도 taxonomy or proof of legal equivalence; selected articles must be loaded before legal-structure or design-suitability claims. The context bundle composes those interfaces into one staged loading surface for Claude, while preserving deferred lookups, ambiguities, WebSearch gaps, and conditionally loaded interpretation/judicial detail when the first bundle needs it; when eager-loaded authority detail references or reviews different articles than the loaded target article, the bundle emits `authority_article_mismatch` gaps, when structured article references are absent it emits `authority_article_unverified` gaps, and when those references match only some requested articles it emits `authority_article_partial_match` gaps for the missing requested articles. These states leave target-article authority claims to scoped follow-up searches unless the structured references already match the requested article. Institutional-system loading reuses that bundle shape for an explicit statute set, records the input statutes in `request.statute_ids`, and preserves `request.as_of` plus `not_effective_as_of` gaps when an explicit statute is not effective on the reference date. Delegated-criteria loading uses the same statute anchor but additionally fills `loaded.administrative_rules` and `loaded.annex_forms` for bounded selected operational criteria, while preserving failed or unselected detail as gaps/deferred lookups.

Administrative-rule back-references are deliberately conservative. MOLEG-API can surface the delegating statute/article from explicit `admrul` metadata such as 위임/근거/수권/상위 법령ㆍ조문 fields or an explicitly named `위임근거` field, but it does not parse ordinary body text, change reasons, or run reverse delegation lookups to fabricate missing links.

## Answering Discipline For The Skill

- Never describe a proposed bill as current law unless MOLEG-API or another authoritative enacted-law source proves it.
- Never describe what an enacted bill changed from current-law text or bridge resolution alone; load history/diff first.
- When `compare_law_versions()` is loaded, limit amendment-delta claims to the selected before/after rows. Do not infer legislative intent, amendment reason, full bill purpose, or every changed provision from `oldAndNew` alone.
- Never cite 구법 or 폐지법 text as current-law authority. Carry the historical as-of date and preserved history/repeal status in the answer.
- State whether a law text was retrieved by effective-date or promulgation-date basis when the distinction matters.
- Cite source type and identity metadata with legal text.
- Cite `supplementary_provisions` separately from main articles when discussing 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치. Do not answer transition scope from law-level `effective_date` metadata or a main article alone.
- Cite `AdministrativeRuleText.supplementary_provisions` separately from administrative-rule articles when discussing rule 시행일, 적용례, or 경과조치. Do not answer rule transition scope from `AdministrativeRuleText.identity.effective_date` metadata or an administrative-rule article alone.
- Use `ArticleText.text` / `AdministrativeRuleArticleText.text` for article substance. Do not summarize definitions, exceptions, application targets, or requirements from `조문제목` or top-level `조문내용` alone, because nested 항, 호, and 목 can carry the operative text.
- Check `is_deleted`, `revision_type`, `moved_from`, `moved_to`, and `has_changes` before treating a loaded article as operative current text. A deleted article can be cited as deleted source state, not as a current rule. A moved article can be cited as movement source state; use `load_article_context()` to load the destination article before current-substance claims.
- Check `AdministrativeRuleArticleText.is_deleted`, `revision_type`, and `moved_to` before treating administrative-rule article text as current operational criteria. A deleted administrative-rule article is source status; a moved article needs `load_administrative_rule_context().current_articles` before destination criteria can be cited.
- Use `load_authority_context()` when the prompt asks for interpretation, case, or Constitutional Court authority for specific statute articles. Cite `current_authorities`, not merely `loaded`, for current target-article authority claims.
- Use `referenced_articles` and `reviewed_articles` on interpretation, case, and Constitutional Court detail results to filter by article before spending reasoning budget on full free-text review.
- Do not cite a loaded interpretation, court case, or Constitutional Court decision as authority for the user's target article unless its structured `referenced_articles` or `reviewed_articles` match that law/article. If loaded authority details point to different articles, disclose that mismatch and run follow-up authority searches scoped to the target article before making a target-article authority claim.
- Do not cite Constitutional Court holdings, summaries, reviewed statutes, referenced statutes, or full text from `search_constitutional_decisions()` alone; use `get_constitutional_decision()` for the selected decision first.
- Surface ambiguity and no-result states clearly.
- Move to WebSearch for latest non-legal facts instead of forcing MOLEG-API to answer them.
- Do not cite MOLEG legal text as support for latest statistics, news, announcements, or social facts; cite WebSearch sources separately for that part of the answer.
- When annex/form candidates appear, mention them as possible operative attached material unless the answer has actually inspected the selected body through `get_annex_form_body()` or another authoritative source.
- Do not cite administrative-rule article text, criteria, supplementary provisions, or current operational criteria from `search_administrative_rules()` alone.
- Do not cite annex/form thresholds, amounts, criteria, form contents, body text, or extracted rows from `search_annex_forms()` alone.
- Treat an empty `search_annex_forms()` result as scoped query/source/type/scope evidence only. Do not answer that no attached criteria, annex, form, threshold table, amount criterion, or attached material exists until source text, alternate annex/form terms, administrative-rule sources, or selected detail paths have been checked and disclosed.
- Do not treat empty `structured_data.rows` as proof that a loaded annex/form has no thresholds or criteria when `parsing_confidence` is low; use the retained plain text or request manual table inspection.
- Treat `expand_legal_query()` output as candidate planning context. It can suggest terms, laws, articles, and WebSearch follow-ups, but it is not a source to cite as final authority. No legal-basis claim is allowed until selected law/article text has been loaded.
- Treat `load_legal_context_bundle()` as source loading, not legal reasoning. Claude still decides what each loaded source means and which deferred lookups to run; eager-loaded interpretation/case/Constitutional Court detail is a bounded first pass, not an exhaustive authority survey. Do not claim exhaustive interpretation, case, Constitutional Court, administrative-rule, or annex/form coverage from the bundle alone.
- When `load_legal_context_bundle()` emits `authority_article_mismatch` gaps, treat eager-loaded authority details as follow-up search context, not target-article authority. Only the loaded target article text can be cited until scoped authority searches match `referenced_articles` or `reviewed_articles` to the target article.
- When `load_legal_context_bundle()` emits `authority_article_unverified` gaps, treat missing structured article references as unknown source state, not an implicit match. Run scoped authority searches or inspect selected detail references before citing eager-loaded authority detail for the target article.
- When `load_legal_context_bundle()` emits `authority_article_partial_match` gaps, treat eager-loaded authority details as authority only for the requested articles they structurally reference or review. Run scoped follow-up authority searches for the missing requested articles before citing those details for them.
- When `load_legal_context_bundle()` or `load_authority_context()` emits `authority_temporal_mismatch` gaps, treat matching `referenced_articles` or `reviewed_articles` as historical or date-unverified context, not current-authority proof. Run `trace_law_history()` or load the target article as of the authority date before saying the authority reflects current wording.
- Treat `load_institutional_system()` as composition over a statute set Claude already chose. If the statute set is uncertain, run `search_laws()` / `expand_legal_query()` first and surface ambiguity instead of asking MOLEG-API to infer the 제도. Do not call the returned statute set exhaustive unless separate discovery was run and disclosed. If the question is current-force scoped, pass `as_of` and disclose any `not_effective_as_of` gap for individual statutes.
- Treat `get_law_structure()` output as hierarchy source context only. It may support a statement that an enforcement decree/rule or administrative rule appears under a statute, but not that a specific article delegates to it or that its body/operational criteria were inspected.
- Treat empty `find_delegated_rules()` / `NoResultError` states as scoped law/article evidence only. Do not answer that no delegated rule, subordinate rule, notice, annex, or delegated criteria exists until law structure, alternate article scopes, administrative-rule sources, or annex/forms have been checked and disclosed.
- When `load_delegated_criteria()` emits `delegated_criteria_source_mismatch` or `delegated_criteria_source_unverified`, treat the loaded administrative-rule detail as follow-up context, not target-article operational criteria. Its explicit source law/article must match the target statute/article before citation as delegated criteria.
- Treat `search_laws()` output as identity candidate metadata. Load selected source text with `get_law()` or `get_article()` before citing article wording, current duties, sanctions, or procedures.
- Treat MOLEG official interpretations and ministry first-instance interpretations as different authority levels. Search metadata can support authority labeling, but load selected interpretation detail before discussing legal substance.
- Treat `search_interpretations()` output as interpretation candidate metadata. Load selected detail with `get_interpretation()` before citing 질의요지, 회답, 이유, 관련법령, or the interpretation's legal substance.
- Treat `search_cases()` output as court-case candidate metadata. Load selected detail with `get_case()` before citing 판시사항, 판결요지, 참조조문, 참조판례, full text, or the court's legal rule.
- Treat an empty `search_cases()` result as scoped query/court/body evidence only. Do not answer that no relevant precedent, court case, judicial authority, or judicial reasoning exists until alternate terms, court scopes, Constitutional Court decisions, or interpretations have been checked and disclosed.
- Treat an empty `search_administrative_rules()` result as scoped query/filter evidence only. Do not answer that no delegated criteria, subordinate rule, notice, or annex exists until delegation, law structure, alternate rule names, or annex/form sources have been checked and disclosed.
- Treat selected administrative-rule detail as loaded source text, not automatically current law. If `AdministrativeRuleText.identity.effective_date` is after the answer's reference date, disclose that future effective date and avoid current-operational-criteria language until a currently effective rule is loaded.
- Treat Constitutional Court search as candidate discovery plus selected detail loading. Use loaded `reviewed_articles` for citations, but do not claim exhaustive doctrine coverage or "no constitutional risk" from free-text `detc` search alone.
- Treat an empty `search_constitutional_decisions()` result as scoped query/body evidence only. Do not answer that no constitutional decision, constitutional authority, or constitutional risk exists until alternate doctrine terms, reviewed-statute terms, ordinary court cases, or interpretations have been checked and disclosed.
- Treat `find_comparable_mechanisms()` output as candidate planning context. It can suggest statutes and article anchors for comparison, but the skill must inspect selected articles with `get_article()` before describing source structure, saying the mechanisms are legally equivalent, or recommending them as appropriate for a new bill.
